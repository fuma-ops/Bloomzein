## Narration scripts (record these as the English audio)

**1. Neck & Shoulder Rolls** (`neck-shoulder-rolls.mp3`)
> Sit tall and soften the jaw. Slowly circle the shoulders back a few times, then forward. Let the head drift ear toward shoulder, and roll it slowly through centre to the other side. Move with your breath, unhurried.

**2. Thread the Needle** (`thread-the-needle.mp3`)
> From all fours, reach the right arm up, then thread it under the left, shoulder and cheek resting on the mat. Let the left hand stay planted or reach overhead. Breathe into the space between the shoulder blades.

**3. Bird Dog** (`bird-dog.mp3`)
> On all fours, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips. Reach the right arm forward and the left leg back, long and level. Keep the belly drawn in and the hips steady. Hold, then lower with control.

**4. Standing Side Stretch** (`standing-side-stretch.mp3`)
> Stand tall and sweep both arms overhead. Clasp the hands, and on an exhale lean gently to the right, opening the left side of the waist. Keep both feet grounded. Breathe into the long side body.

**5. High Lunge** (`high-lunge.mp3`)
> Step the right foot forward, back heel lifted high. Bend the front knee over the ankle, back leg strong and straight. Sweep the arms overhead, lengthen the spine, and lift the chest. Steady and bright.

**6. Twisted Crescent Lunge** (`crescent-twist.mp3`)
> From a high lunge with the right foot forward, bring the hands to heart. Exhale and hook the left elbow outside the right knee, twisting from the belly. Press the palms together, open the chest, and gaze past the top shoulder.

**7. Extended Side Angle** (`extended-side-angle.mp3`)
> From Warrior II with the right knee bent, lower the right forearm onto the thigh, or the hand to the floor. Reach the left arm over the ear, drawing one long line from the back heel to the fingertips. Open the chest to the sky.

**8. Pyramid Pose** (`pyramid.mp3`)
> Step the feet a leg's length apart, both hips facing the front foot. Keep the front leg straight, hinge at the hips, and fold over the front thigh with a long spine. Let the head release toward the shin.

**9. Revolved Triangle** (`revolved-triangle.mp3`)
> From a short stance with the right foot forward, lengthen the spine and place the left hand to the floor or a block outside the front foot. Twist open, reaching the right arm to the sky. Keep both legs strong and the hips level.

**10. Goddess Pose** (`goddess.mp3`)
> Step the feet wide, toes turned out. Bend the knees and sink the hips down toward knee height, knees tracking over the toes. Lift the chest, and either cactus the arms or rest the hands at heart. Feel strong and rooted.

**11. Wide-Legged Forward Fold** (`wide-legged-forward-fold.mp3`)
> Step the feet wide and parallel. Hinge at the hips and fold forward, hands to the floor between the feet. Let the crown of the head release toward the mat and the neck stay soft. Breathe into the backs of the legs.

**12. Warrior III** (`warrior-3.mp3`)
> From standing, shift your weight onto the right foot. Hinge forward and float the left leg back until the body forms one long line, parallel to the floor. Reach the arms forward or back alongside the body. Fix your gaze and breathe.

**13. Eagle Pose** (`eagle.mp3`)
> Bend the knees softly. Cross the right thigh over the left, hooking the foot if it's available. Wrap the left arm under the right, palms reaching toward each other. Sink and lift at once, drawing everything to the midline.

**14. Dancer's Pose** (`dancer.mp3`)
> Standing on the right foot, bend the left knee and catch the inside of the left ankle behind you. Reach the right arm forward, then press the foot into the hand and lift the chest, blooming open. Keep the gaze soft and steady.

**15. Standing Figure Four** (`standing-figure-four.mp3`)
> Standing on the left foot, cross the right ankle over the left thigh, flexing the foot. Sit the hips back and down as if into a chair, keeping the chest lifted. Hands come to heart. Breathe into the outer right hip.

**16. Sphinx Pose** (`sphinx.mp3`)
> Lie on your belly and set the forearms on the mat, elbows under the shoulders. Press down and lift the chest into a gentle backbend. Draw the shoulders away from the ears and lengthen the low back. Soft and steady.

**17. Locust Pose** (`locust.mp3`)
> Lie on your belly, arms alongside the body, palms down. On an inhale, lift the chest, arms, and legs away from the floor, reaching back through the toes. Keep the neck long and the glutes soft. Breathe steadily.

**18. Bow Pose** (`bow.mp3`)
> On your belly, bend both knees and reach back to catch the ankles. Kick the feet into the hands to lift the chest and thighs, rocking gently on the belly. Breathe into the front of the body. Release slowly.

**19. Upward-Facing Dog** (`upward-dog.mp3`)
> From lying face down, place the hands beside the ribs and press into them, straightening the arms and lifting the chest and thighs off the mat. Roll the shoulders back and down, and lengthen the neck. Only the hands and tops of the feet touch.

**20. Fish Pose** (`fish.mp3`)
> Lie on your back, hands tucked under the hips, palms down. Press into the forearms to lift the chest high, and let the crown of the head rest lightly on the mat. Open the throat and heart, and breathe into the chest. Come up with care.

**21. Lizard Pose** (`lizard.mp3`)
> From a low lunge with the right foot forward, walk the foot to the outer edge of the mat and bring both hands inside the front foot. Stay on the hands, or lower to the forearms. Let the front hip open and breathe low and slow.

**22. Happy Baby** (`happy-baby.mp3`)
> Lie on your back and draw both knees toward the armpits. Hold the outer edges of the feet, soles facing up, and gently rock side to side. Let the low back soften into the mat. Breathe and release the hips.

**23. Cow Face Pose** (`cow-face.mp3`)
> Sit and stack the right knee over the left, feet beside the hips. Reach the right arm up and bend it down the back; sweep the left arm behind to clasp the fingers, or use a strap. Lift the chest tall and breathe.

**24. Frog Pose** (`frog.mp3`)
> From all fours, widen the knees out to the sides, inner ankles and shins on the mat, feet flexed. Lower onto the forearms and let the hips sink back gently. This is intense — go slow, breathe deep, and never force it.

**25. Reclined Figure Four** (`reclined-figure-four.mp3`)
> Lie on your back, knees bent. Cross the right ankle over the left thigh, flexing the foot. Thread the hands behind the left thigh and gently draw the legs toward you. Keep the head heavy and breathe into the outer hip.

**26. Forearm Plank** (`forearm-plank.mp3`)
> Set the forearms on the mat, elbows under the shoulders, and step back into a straight line from heels to crown. Draw the belly in, tuck the tailbone gently, and press the floor away. Breathe steadily and hold strong.

**27. Low Plank** (`chaturanga.mp3`)
> From a high plank, shift forward onto the toes and lower halfway down, elbows hugging the ribs at ninety degrees. Keep the body one firm line and the shoulders level with the elbows. Hold a breath, then flow on.

**28. Dead Bug** (`dead-bug.mp3`)
> Lie on your back, arms reaching to the ceiling, knees stacked over the hips at ninety degrees. Press the low back into the mat. Slowly lower the right arm and left leg, then return, and switch. Move with control and steady breath.

**29. Hollow Body Hold** (`hollow-hold.mp3`)
> Lie on your back and press the low back firmly into the mat. Lift the head, shoulders, arms, and legs a few inches off the floor, reaching long in both directions. Keep the belly drawn in and the breath calm. Hold your strong little boat.

**30. Modified Side Plank** (`modified-side-plank.mp3`)
> From your right forearm with the bottom knee down, stack the shoulder over the elbow and lift the hips into a gentle side line. Reach the top arm to the sky and open the chest. Steady breath — then switch sides.

**31. Reverse Plank** (`reverse-plank.mp3`)
> Sit with the legs long and place the hands behind the hips, fingers pointing forward. Press down and lift the hips high, reaching the toes toward the floor. Open the chest and let the head drop back if it feels good. Breathe into the whole front line.

**32. Seated Wide-Leg Fold** (`seated-wide-leg-fold.mp3`)
> Sit and open the legs wide, toes and knees pointing up. Sit tall through the spine, then walk the hands forward and fold from the hips, keeping the chest long. Go only as far as the breath stays smooth.

**33. Ragdoll Fold** (`ragdoll.mp3`)
> From standing, feet hip-width, fold forward and bend the knees generously. Take hold of opposite elbows and let the head and neck hang completely heavy. Sway a little side to side, releasing the whole spine.

**34. Gate Pose** (`gate-pose.mp3`)
> Kneel, then extend the right leg out to the side, foot flat, toes forward. Rest the right hand lightly on the leg and sweep the left arm up and over, opening the left side of the body. Breathe into the long side waist. Then switch.

**35. Supported Bridge** (`supported-bridge.mp3`)
> Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Lift the hips and slide a block or cushion under the sacrum, then rest your whole weight down onto it. Let the arms fall open and the breath slow. Stay and be held.

**36. Banana Pose** (`banana-pose.mp3`)
> Lie on your back and walk both feet and the upper body over to the right, keeping the hips grounded, so the body curves like a crescent. Clasp the hands overhead if it feels good. Breathe into the long stretched left side. Then switch.

**37. Supported Rest** (`supported-savasana.mp3`)
> Lie down and slide a bolster or rolled blanket under the knees, and a soft layer under the head. Let the feet fall open, palms face up, and the whole body grow heavy. There is nothing to do now but breathe and be.

**38. Alternate Nostril Breath** (`alternate-nostril.mp3`)
> Sit tall and rest the left hand on the knee. With the right thumb, gently close the right nostril and breathe in through the left. Close the left, release the right, and breathe out. Continue, guiding the breath slow and even through one nostril at a time.

**39. Box Breathing** (`box-breathing.mp3`)
> Sit comfortably and let the eyes close. Breathe in for a count of four, hold gently for four, breathe out for four, and stay empty for four. Keep each side of the breath even and smooth, like tracing the four sides of a square.
